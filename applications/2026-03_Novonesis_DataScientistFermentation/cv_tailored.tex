    \section{Relevant Experience}

\begin{onecolentry}

\textbf{Machine Learning \& Statistical Modelling} \\
At Novo Nordisk, my thesis focused on developing statistical methods for assessing batch variability in single-cell RNA-seq data from cell therapy manufacturing. I developed the Batch Mixing Index (BMI), a novel metric with permutation-based significance testing, and built scBatch, a Python package integrating seven complementary metrics. I also explored state-of-the-art ML approaches by applying deep learning models to embed batches into a shared latent space. This work gave me hands-on experience with biological data, predictive modelling, and translating complex datasets into actionable insights.

        \vspace{0.3 cm}

\textbf{Large-Scale Data Pipelines \& Research Software} \\
At DTU HealthTech, I built and optimized bioinformatics pipelines processing different omics on HPC infrastructure. I co-developed IsoformSwitchAnalyzeR v2, an R package now published on bioRxiv and used by researchers worldwide. I work daily with Python and R, and I am comfortable with data integration, statistical analysis, and building tools that others can depend on. I have also taken ML and Deep Learning courses at DTU, applying these methods in real research settings.

        \vspace{0.3 cm}

\textbf{Biotech Background \& Industry Experience} \\
My academic background spans biotechnology (BSc) and bioinformatics (MSc), giving me a solid foundation in biological systems. My six months at Novo Nordisk gave me direct exposure to industrial biotech workflows, cross-functional collaboration with R\&D and CMC teams, and understanding how data science supports manufacturing decisions. I understand the biological context behind fermentation processes and can bridge the gap between data models and experimental reality.
