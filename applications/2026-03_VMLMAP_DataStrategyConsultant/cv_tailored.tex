    \section{Relevant Experience}

\begin{onecolentry}

\textbf{Data Translation \& Client-Facing Communication} \\
At Illuminera (IQVIA), I worked directly with pharmaceutical clients to turn data into strategic recommendations. That meant conducting market and competitor analyses, running a social media listening project across Chinese health platforms (RedNote, hospital communities), and presenting findings via Power BI and PowerPoint to senior stakeholders. Getting the translation right between data and business decision was the core of the job, and it is what I enjoyed most about consulting.

        \vspace{0.3 cm}

\textbf{Quantitative Analysis \& Research Software} \\
Two years at DTU HealthTech and a thesis at Novo Nordisk gave me a strong quantitative foundation. At Novo, I designed and implemented the Batch Mixing Index (BMI), a metric for detecting systematic variation in large datasets, validated with permutation-based statistical testing in Python. At DTU, I processed more than 40,000 samples on HPC infrastructure and co-developed \href{https://github.com/kvittingseerup/IsoformSwitchAnalyzeR}{IsoformSwitchAnalyzeR} v2, now published on bioRxiv. Working at this scale taught me to build pipelines that are reliable enough for others to depend on.

        \vspace{0.3 cm}

\textbf{Applied AI \& Data-Driven Product Building} \\
Outside research, I build tools that solve real problems with data. \href{https://github.com/chunxubioinfor/DailyJobMatch}{DailyJobMatch} is an AI agent I built that fetches, filters, and ranks job postings daily using LLM-based scoring. I also built a full-stack job tracker (Next.js, Supabase) with a multi-user pipeline and file storage. These projects reflect how I think about data products: the model is rarely the hard part --- designing the system so the output is actually useful and trusted is.

\end{onecolentry}
