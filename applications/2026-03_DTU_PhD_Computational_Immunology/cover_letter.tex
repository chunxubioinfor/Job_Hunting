\lettercontent{Dear Leon,}

\lettercontent{
I am applying for the PhD scholarship in Computational Immunology and Machine Learning at DTU Health Tech. I recently completed my MSc in Bioinformatics and Systems Biology at DTU, where I have spent the past two and a half years working on methods development and software for high-dimensional omics data. When I read the position description, the core tasks — designing ML frameworks for high-dimensional sequence data, rigorous model benchmarking, evaluating transferability across datasets — felt like a natural continuation of what I have already been building towards.
}

\lettercontent{
My master's thesis, carried out at Novo Nordisk, focused on developing and benchmarking computational methods to assess batch-to-batch variability in single-cell RNA-seq data. This involved designing quantitative metrics from scratch, systematically comparing existing computational tools, and exploring ML approaches to connect transcriptomic signals with functional outcomes — all packaged into \textbf{scBatch}, a reproducible Python framework. Alongside this, I spent two years as a research assistant at DTU HealthTech contributing to \href{https://github.com/kvittingseerup/IsoformSwitchAnalyzeR}{IsoformSwitchAnalyzeR} v2, a tool for isoform-level analysis of long-read and single-cell sequencing data, which resulted in a published preprint. I am used to building things that are not just functional, but transferable and well-documented — which I see as central to this PhD's focus on generalisability across datasets and biological contexts.
}

\lettercontent{
I want to be transparent: I do not have a background in immunology or TCR biology specifically. But I have come to see that as a feature rather than a limitation for this position. The role is explicitly about methodological innovation — developing and rigorously evaluating ML models for high-dimensional sequence data — and that is precisely the kind of work I have been doing, just applied to a different biological context. I am genuinely excited by the clinical angle of this project, particularly the connection to type 1 diabetes and the collaboration with Rigshospitalet. It is the kind of translational motivation that I found energising during my time at Novo Nordisk, and I would like to continue working in that direction.
}

\lettercontent{
Thank you very much for considering my application. I would be happy to discuss the project further.
}
