\lettercontent{My interest in cancer research has developed gradually but consistently. During my time in China, cancer is often spoken about indirectly, as it is closely associated with fear and loss. I have seen many patients and families share their situations publicly to raise funds for treatment, and whenever possible, I have tried to help in small ways. Over time, this also shaped how I think about my own work. I realised that, beyond sympathy, the most meaningful contribution I can make is through my skills. This is one reason why, when choosing datasets and projects, I am repeatedly drawn to cancer-related questions. Most recently, we applied isoform-level analysis to single-cell glioblastoma data, where we observed changes in isoform usage that may be linked to the tumour invasion. Experiences like this have reinforced my motivation to work on cancer from a computational perspective.
}
